Arcade Project Summary

The purpose of Galaga is to be able to fight off the enemy bugs from
destroying your starship. With limited amount of bullets and an increasing
rate of enemy bugs appearing, you must keep fighting until you either
run out of bullets or destroy your ship. We focused on using the scripts
that sent messages out to other sprites and the variable "game_over", which
could be triggered in countless ways from losing all the bullets, running
into an enemy, or losing all your lives. These scripts allow the use of
the final sprite to trigger both the notification that the game has to
players and the "stop" script that concludes the game, thus the player has
to click the green flag to restart the game. We encountered glitches from
the Scratch itself with the positions of the axes being offset, finding
scripts that overlapped each other causing multiple triggers, and the
best method to clean the scripts to allow us to see the progress and the
necessary fixes to our game.

Story Reflection

Our story begins with a teenage boy, William, who goes to school to
find a girl that he loves and tries to kiss her, but things completely
go wrong. He finds himself kidnapped and placed in an insane asylum where
he must find a way out. He goes through different obstacles and has
to make quick decisions in order to survive from killing to going through
shady hallways.

a) We looked into "meme"ing up our project and finding the best ways to
make the story as creative, whacky, and logical as possible. We had to
understand how Python works and the different methods of organization to
allow the story to start and move the plot correctly.

b) After having our lesson on organizing our story in Python, we only had
the problem of allowing the story to start when we press running instead of
typing out "introduction()". To solve this problem, we learned to place the
first command to start the story at the end, so when the program runs, the first
command it reads automatically is "introduction()", instead of the user having
to define it.

c) I liked this project since it taught me how to organize and label the
different commands on my project more carefully. I disliked the creative
portion of the project, but that is also why the project is able to move
forward.

d) I liked Phillip's "Cynical". The story was very concise and short,
yet the plot of the story was very interesting and you felt that any actions
you chose morally affected how you perceive of yourself.

e) I would change my story to make it more serious and much darker
since some of the class favorites were very dark yet intriguing to play.
